The Problem

High earners lose REP status
not because they didn't work —
because they can't prove it.

The IRS places the entire burden of proof on you. Without a contemporaneous, organized record, even legitimate participation gets denied in audit.

01
No contemporaneous log
Reconstructed logs created at tax time are routinely rejected by IRS examiners. Courts have consistently ruled against taxpayers who couldn't show daily or weekly records.
02
Investor hours counted by mistake
Reviewing P&Ls, attending investor meetings, monitoring performance — none of these count. Taxpayers routinely inflate hours with investor activities that the IRS specifically excludes.
03
W-2 job kills the 50% test
A physician working 2,000 W-2 hours needs 2,001+ RE hours just to pass the 50% test. Most high earners fail here and never realize it until audit.

Built for High-Income Earners

The stakes are different
when your W-2 is $500K

The higher your income, the more the IRS scrutinizes your REP claim — and the more documentation quality matters. RepTrack is built for the scenarios that actually affect high earners.

$
NIIT elimination
At $600K W-2 income + $80K rental income, you owe $3,040/yr in NIIT alone. REP status eliminates it — but only with documentation to back the claim.
⊘
$25K offset is worthless above $150K MAGI
The passive loss allowance phases out completely at $150K MAGI. Every high earner needs full REP status — not the active participation workaround.
⚡
Cost segregation without REP = trapped losses
Cost segregation generates large paper losses — but without REP status above $150K MAGI, they're suspended indefinitely. RepTrack helps you prove the status that unlocks them.
